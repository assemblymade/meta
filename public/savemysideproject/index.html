Save Your Side Project

The Assembly community collaboratively builds and runs software products. Anyone can share an idea. The community collaborates to build the best ones, and every month Assembly distributes a product's revenue to everyone who contributed to it.

Already more than 4 million people use products built by the Assembly community. To usher in 2015, we're making it easier to get the help of the Assembly community on your existing side project. Have something anywhere between a half-baked idea and the next Snapchat? Bring it to Assembly and the community can help it thrive.

Always be shipping

Instead of a project stalling for days, weeks, or even months "when life happens," the community is here to help you keep momentum. It's great to wake up to find new designs or pull requests and be a little closer to launching.

Collective minds are better

Everything is open so anyone can jump in and help out — benefiting your side project whether it is in need of design, development, marketing, or simply feedback.

Profit

Reward contributions with ownership and share the success with everyone who made it happen. If your product starts earning money, Assembly splits the profits with everyone who had a part.

Assembly also covers the bills for hosting and domains while you focus on growing the product and making more people happy with it.

"I knew I had a great idea, it was solving my needs, but it didn't seem to capture the people. I moved it to Assembly and worked with the community to create a brand, package the product, and improve our features. Fast forward six months and we were celebrating as Runbook spent a day on top of Hacker News and Product Hunt — and now my side project is bringing in revenue." - Ben Cordell, Runbook.io

Common questions

  • What kind of side project is good for this?

    Anything that might solve a problem for someone in the world. Even if you don't see it scaling to a massive customer base, you might be able to help some people out and make some money from your hard work.

  • What if my side project is only half-built?

    Thats OK! Lots of products have gone from idea to launch on Assembly — it's a perfect place to get development on your product moving.

  • What will happen if I put my side project on Assembly?

    You'll have the chance to bring people along to work on it, and talented people from the Assembly community might be interested in working with you as well. If you or anyone else in the community figures out how to monetize your side project, the revenue will go to you and other contributors.

  • Who pays the product expenses?

    Assembly will cover these basic costs before a product launches. At times members of the community will pay for them too. In any case, these expenditures will be tracked and reimbursed when the product earns profits.

    Once a product is live and earning revenue, monthly costs are paid before community earnings are distributed.

  • Am I giving up control of my product by putting it on Assembly?

    No. You start as the only member of your product's Core Team. Core Teams make all decisions regarding quality control, vision, and which contributions get merged into the product.

    Over time, you are welcome to add people to the Core Team. (We can help you navigate these decisions; don't hesitate to reach out).

  • Who owns the intellectual property?

    You do. However, all products on Assembly are licensed under AGPL, and by agreeing to our terms of service you also are granting non-exclusive rights to Assembly to monetize this IP on behalf of the community and you.

    The right to monetize the IP is important so that Assembly can accept payments and distribute this revenue to you and your contributors.

  • What are Assembly's platform fees?

    Building on Assembly connects you with others around the world. Together, you build products without having to worry about finances, legal fees, and all the other burdens of operating a traditional product. For this, the platform collects a 10% platform fee of the revenue after expenses. You can see Coderwall as an example of a mature product paying out in this model. We’re continuing to invest in creating new value for the community as well as exploring ways to reduce fees if feasible.
